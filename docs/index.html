1 · Executive Summary

Between 06 April and 29 April 2026, AccessRP received 1,089 real customer complaints from 517 unique users (after filtering out automation, delivery-failure, and submission-confirmation tickets). Average customer sentiment was 44.1/100.

Tickets clustered into 8 high-level issue categories, with the largest share belonging to Business / Process (483 tickets) and Language / UX (159).

Three findings stakeholders should act on

  1. Volume: Lease / tenancy is the most-reported issue (249 tickets, 145 users affected). Top services involved: Lease, Company management, Owner Services.
  2. Severity: Error / system issue drives the angriest tickets in the period (avg sentiment 31). It is concentrated in Lease, Transaction, Developer Services.
  3. Service hotspot: Lease accounts for 319 of the 1,089 tickets (29% of total) — any improvement here has the largest immediate volume impact.

Methodology, ticket-ID conventions, and PII handling are noted in the footer.

Methodology. Source data: AccessRP Freshdesk export for the period covered. Tickets in the “Ads Test Tickets and Auto-Emails” service and tickets matching auto-reply / delivery-failure / submission-confirmation patterns are excluded from this analysis. Themes are derived via a rule-based classifier applied to subject and description text.

Ticket IDs. Numeric IDs are Freshdesk's internal ticket numbers and are meaningless without access to the helpdesk.

PII handling. Verbatim quotes have been redacted: email addresses, phone numbers, EID-style identifiers, application reference numbers, unit numbers, licence numbers, passport numbers, and URLs are replaced with bracketed placeholders. Customer names that appear inside the prose body are not automatically masked — review before publishing if your dataset contains names embedded in free-text descriptions.
